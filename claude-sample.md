# Customer Journey: Post-Purchase Engagement Layer

## Strategic Context

### Situation
The store converts first-time buyers well but has no post-purchase engagement — no loyalty mechanism, no personalized re-engagement, no structured upsell path. Repeat purchases happen by chance.

### Problem
Customers who bought once aren't coming back. Cart abandonment sits around 72%, emails are generic blasts, and there's no recommendation engine. Customer lifetime value is well below benchmark.

### Implication
The store competes purely on price and ad spend. Rising acquisition costs erode margins while competitors with loyalty programmes capture the repeat business this store should own.

### Focus
A post-purchase engagement layer — automated emails, a loyalty programme, and personalized recommendations — to raise repeat purchase rate, lift average order value, and build a retained customer base.

## Journey Steps

### Step 1: Browse & Discover
**Walkthrough**: A first-time visitor lands via paid social or organic search and browses category and product pages. The experience is clean but generic — no personalization and no dynamically surfaced social proof.

**What Exists**: Standard product catalogue and site search.

**What's Needed**: Dynamic product recommendations, review highlights, and 'customers also viewed' carousels.

### Step 2: Add to Cart & Checkout
**Walkthrough**: Customer adds items and proceeds to checkout. Guest checkout is available but there is no email capture for abandonment recovery and no upsell prompt at this stage.

**What Exists**: Functional checkout flow with guest option.

**What's Needed**: Pre-checkout email capture, cart abandonment recovery trigger, and a 'frequently bought together' upsell module.

### Step 3: Order Confirmation & First Post-Purchase Touch
**Walkthrough**: After purchase the customer receives a standard transactional confirmation email with no brand story, no loyalty invite, and no cross-sell.

**What Exists**: Basic transactional order confirmation email.

**What's Needed**: Branded confirmation email with loyalty programme invite, live tracking link, and one contextually relevant product recommendation.

### Step 4: Delivery & Unboxing
**Walkthrough**: Product arrives with no in-box material and no triggered post-delivery email to confirm satisfaction or prompt a review.

**What Exists**: Standard shipping and fulfilment process.

**What's Needed**: Post-delivery email on day +2 requesting a review, awarding loyalty points for completion, and surfacing a complementary product.

### Step 5: Loyalty & Re-engagement
**Walkthrough**: Repeat purchase is left entirely to chance. There is no loyalty programme, no milestone communication, and no win-back sequence for lapsed customers.

**What Exists**: n/a — nothing currently exists for post-purchase retention.

**What's Needed**: Loyalty points engine, automated re-engagement at 30/60/90 days, win-back offer for lapsed customers, and a points-expiry nudge email.
